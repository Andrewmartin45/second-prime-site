His cardiologist watched the numbers for three years. We moved them in six months.

“My cardiologist had been watching my ApoB and Lp(a) for three years. Nothing moved it. Six months here did.”

Where he started

A 52-year-old CEO with a family history of heart disease and a cardiologist who had been “monitoring” his elevated markers for years without a plan to change them. He wanted action, not another wait-and-see appointment.

What we found

ApoB and Lp(a) both elevated, both genuine cardiovascular risk, both untouched by years of observation. The difference wasn’t more testing. It was a protocol built to actually move the numbers.

The plan

Aggressive lipid management coordinated with physician oversight, blood pressure work, and a metabolic reset. Quarterly re-testing held the protocol accountable to the numbers, not the calendar.
