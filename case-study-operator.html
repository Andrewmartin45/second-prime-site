He had a cardiologist, a trainer, and a nutritionist. None of them talked.

“I had a cardiologist, a trainer, a nutritionist. None of them talked to each other. I was the one connecting the dots.”

Where he started

A 46-year-old asset manager who had assembled a team of specialists and was still doing the integration himself, in his spare time, with no expertise. Each expert optimized their slice. Nobody owned the whole picture.

What we found

Fragmented care had left real risk unaddressed: high particle count, rising inflammation, and a metabolic profile trending the wrong way. The data existed across four providers. Nobody had ever read it together.

The plan

One integrated protocol replacing four disconnected ones. Nutrition, training, supplements, and clinical care under a single plan, run by a team that talks to each other. He went back to running his fund.
